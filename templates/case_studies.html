High-throughput Simulations and Machine Learning for Materials Design and Discovery

Development of data-driven insights and structure-property relationships are essential to reduce the search space and guide efforts toward selection of outstanding materials. At Monoclinic, we use molecular simulations and machine learning to rapidly screen hundreds of thousands materials to significantly speed up the way MOFs are discovered for energy and environment applications including toxic gas sensing, CO2 capture and hydrogen storage.

IoT enabled MOF Sensors

Our innovative product can be applied to a MOF-IoT Gas Detection System. It combines advanced nanoporous Metal-Organic Framework (MOF) adsorbents with a state-of-the-art Internet of Things (IoT) wireless sensor network to detect a wide range of toxic gases. This first-of-its-kind system alerts affected individuals through alarms and communicates directly with relevant authorities, enhancing safety and responsiveness in hazardous situations.

The sensors offer extensive coverage and real-time environmental monitoring, while the mobile application provides instant notifications, actionable recommendations, and options to contact emergency services swiftly.

Designed for use in industrial settings, laboratories, and any areas at risk of toxic gas exposure, our product sets a new standard in environmental safety. Its user-friendly mobile app ensures that all users, regardless of technical skill, can easily manage alerts and respond effectively. Our product not only increases safety by preventing potential disasters but also represents a significant advancement in combining cutting-edge material science with innovative wireless network capabilities.
